# Responsible AI Statement — Credit Cat

Credit Cat is a self-discovery tool for credit-card applicants — not a lender, and not a credit-scoring system. The clearest harm from a misread result is an applicant treating their segment as a verdict: feeling discouraged, or making a financial decision based on a label rather than their full situation. A second risk is misuse — a lender attempting to use the groupings to pre-screen people. The historical approval rates we display also reflect past human lending decisions, which may carry bias. Explicitly **out of scope**: we never predict approval or denial, never output a score, never advise users on how to "game" an application, never function as a lender pre-screen, and never store user data. **Guardrails**: the model is trained only on applicant features — never on the approve/deny label, which we use solely for validation and clearly-caveated context; credit-limit ranges are labeled general industry guidance, not offers; inferred feature meanings are marked as inferred; recommendations are limited to legitimate, changeable behaviors (never traits like age or gender); and all input is processed in-memory and discarded.
